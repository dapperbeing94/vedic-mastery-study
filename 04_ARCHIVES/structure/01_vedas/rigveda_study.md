## Ritual Context

The ·πög Veda was composed primarily for use in the **Soma sacrifice** and other Vedic rituals. The hymns were chanted by priests during elaborate ceremonies designed to maintain cosmic order, propitiate the gods, and secure prosperity for the community.

The central ritual of Vedic religion was the **yaj√±a** (sacrifice), a complex ceremony involving:

- **Offerings** of clarified butter (gh·πõta), grain, and the sacred Soma drink into the sacred fire
- **Invocations** of deities through the recitation of ·πög Vedic hymns
- **Priests** with specialized roles: the Hot·πõ (reciter of ·πög Vedic verses), the Adhvaryu (performer of ritual actions), the UdgƒÅt·πõ (chanter of SƒÅma Vedic melodies), and the Brahman (silent overseer)
