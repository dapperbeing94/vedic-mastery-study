## 1. Introduction

The **Sushruta Samhita** is a foundational Sanskrit text of Ayurveda, the ancient Indian system of medicine. It is a comprehensive treatise on surgery (Shalya Tantra) and is considered one of the most important works in the history of medicine.

## 2. Author and Date

- **Author**: The text is attributed to the sage **Sushruta**, who is known as the "father of surgery."
- **Date**: The Sushruta Samhita is believed to have been composed in its present form between the 6th century BCE and the 4th century CE.

## 3. Structure

The Sushruta Samhita is organized into six sections (Sthanas) and 186 chapters:

| Section | Title | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Sutra Sthana | General principles, surgical instruments, and procedures |
| 2 | Nidana Sthana | Diagnosis of diseases |
| 3 | Sharira Sthana | Anatomy and embryology |
| 4 | Chikitsa Sthana | Therapeutics and treatment of diseases |
| 5 | Kalpa Sthana | Toxicology |
| 6 | Uttara Tantra | Ophthalmology, ENT, and other specialties |

## 4. Core Concepts

The Sushruta Samhita is based on the fundamental principles of Ayurveda, including the Tridosha theory, the Pancha Mahabhuta theory, and the concepts of Dhatus, Malas, and Agni. It also provides a detailed and systematic approach to surgery, including pre-operative, operative, and post-operative procedures.

## 5. Key Contributions

The Sushruta Samhita made significant contributions to the field of surgery, including:

- **Detailed descriptions of surgical procedures**: The text provides detailed descriptions of a wide range of surgical procedures, including plastic surgery, cataract surgery, and rhinoplasty.
- **Classification of surgical instruments**: The text classifies over 100 surgical instruments and provides detailed instructions on their use.
- **Emphasis on anatomy and dissection**: The text emphasizes the importance of anatomical knowledge and provides detailed descriptions of human anatomy based on the dissection of cadavers.

## 6. Significance

The Sushruta Samhita is a landmark text in the history of surgery and has had a profound influence on the development of medicine. It is still widely studied and revered by surgeons and medical historians today.

## 7. Relationship to Other Texts

The Sushruta Samhita is one of the three major texts of the **Brihat Trayi** (the great trio) of Ayurveda, along with the **Charaka Samhita** and the **Ashtanga Hridaya**.
