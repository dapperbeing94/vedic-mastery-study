## 1. Introduction

The **Surya Siddhanta** is an ancient Sanskrit treatise on astronomy. It is a comprehensive work that covers a wide range of topics, from the calculation of planetary positions to the prediction of eclipses.

## 2. Author and Date

- **Author**: The authorship of the Surya Siddhanta is unknown, but it is believed to be a work of the siddhantic tradition of Indian astronomy.
- **Date**: The text is believed to have been composed in its present form between the 4th and 5th centuries CE.

## 3. Structure

The Surya Siddhanta is organized into 14 chapters and contains over 500 verses. The chapters cover a wide range of topics, including:

- The mean motions of the planets
- The true motions of the planets
- The calculation of eclipses
- The precession of the equinoxes
- The dimensions of the earth and the planets

## 4. Core Concepts

The Surya Siddhanta is based on a geocentric model of the universe, with the earth at the center and the sun, moon, and planets revolving around it. The text provides a detailed and systematic approach to the calculation of planetary positions and other astronomical phenomena.

## 5. Significance

The Surya Siddhanta is one of the most important and influential texts of Indian astronomy. It was a primary source for astronomers and mathematicians in India for centuries and had a profound influence on the development of astronomy in other parts of the world.
