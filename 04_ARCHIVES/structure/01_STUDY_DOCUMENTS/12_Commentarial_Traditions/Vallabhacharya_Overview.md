## 3. Philosophical School: Shuddhadvaita

Shuddhadvaita is a non-dualistic school of Vedanta that holds that the ultimate reality (Brahman) is a personal God (Krishna) who is both transcendent and immanent. The individual self (Atman) is a part of Brahman, but it is not identical with Brahman.

## 4. Major Works

Vallabhacharya wrote commentaries on the Brahma Sutras (the **Anubhashya**) and the Bhagavata Purana (the **Subodhini**). He also wrote a number of independent philosophical works, including the **Tattvartha Dipa Nibandha**.

## 5. Core Teachings

- **Brahman as a personal God**: The ultimate reality is a personal God (Krishna) who is the creator, preserver, and destroyer of the universe.
- **Atman as a part of Brahman**: The individual self (Atman) is a part of Brahman, but it is not identical with Brahman.
- **Pushti**: Liberation (moksha) can be achieved through the path of grace (pushti), which is a gift from Krishna.

## 6. Key Contributions

- **Systematization of Shuddhadvaita**: Vallabhacharya systematized and consolidated the teachings of Shuddhadvaita, making it one of the most influential schools of Hindu philosophy.
- **Establishment of the Pushtimarg**: Vallabhacharya established the Pushtimarg, a devotional tradition that has millions of followers around the world.

## 7. Significance

Vallabhacharya is a towering figure in the history of Hindu philosophy. His teachings have had a profound and lasting impact on the intellectual and spiritual landscape of Hinduism, and he is revered as a saint and a philosopher by millions of people around the world.

## 8. Relationship to Other Traditions

Vallabhacharya engaged in debates with scholars of other philosophical schools, including Advaita Vedanta and Dvaita. He was a critic of the impersonalism of Advaita Vedanta and the dualism of Dvaita.
