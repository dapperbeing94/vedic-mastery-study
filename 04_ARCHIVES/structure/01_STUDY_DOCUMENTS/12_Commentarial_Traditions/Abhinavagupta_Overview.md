## 3. Philosophical School: Kashmir Shaivism

Kashmir Shaivism is a non-dualistic school of Hindu philosophy that holds that the ultimate reality (Shiva) is a single, undifferentiated consciousness. The individual self (Atman) is identical with Shiva, and the perceived multiplicity of the world is an illusion (maya).

## 4. Major Works

Abhinavagupta wrote a number of important philosophical works, including the **Tantraloka**, the **Tantrasara**, and the **Paratrisika-Vivarana**. He also wrote a commentary on the Natya Shastra, the ancient Sanskrit treatise on the performing arts.

## 5. Core Teachings

- **Shiva as the only reality**: The ultimate reality is Shiva, which is described as a single, undifferentiated consciousness.
- **Atman is Shiva**: The individual self (Atman) is identical with Shiva.
- **Maya**: The perceived multiplicity of the world is an illusion (maya) that is superimposed on Shiva.
- **Pratyabhijna**: Liberation (moksha) is the recognition (pratyabhijna) of the identity of Atman and Shiva.

## 6. Key Contributions

- **Synthesis of Kashmir Shaivism**: Abhinavagupta systematized and consolidated the teachings of Kashmir Shaivism, making it one of the most influential schools of Hindu philosophy.
- **Aesthetics**: Abhinavagupta made important contributions to the field of aesthetics, and his theory of rasa has had a profound influence on the development of Indian art and literature.

## 8. Relationship to Other Traditions

Abhinavagupta engaged in debates with scholars of other philosophical schools, including Buddhism and Advaita Vedanta. He was a critic of the nihilism of Buddhism and the impersonalism of Advaita Vedanta.
