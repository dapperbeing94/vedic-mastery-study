He is said to have experienced a series of ecstatic visions of Krishna, and he spent the last years of his life in Puri, Odisha, in a state of intense devotional absorption.

## 3. Philosophical School: Achintya Bheda Abheda

Achintya Bheda Abheda is a school of Vedanta that holds that the ultimate reality (Brahman) is both one and different from the individual self (Atman). The relationship between Brahman and Atman is said to be "inconceivable" (achintya), meaning that it cannot be fully understood by the human mind.

## 4. Major Works

Chaitanya Mahaprabhu did not write any major philosophical works himself, but his teachings were recorded by his disciples in a number of important texts, including the **Chaitanya Charitamrita** and the **Chaitanya Bhagavata**.

## 5. Core Teachings

- **Brahman as a personal God**: The ultimate reality is a personal God (Krishna) who is the creator, preserver, and destroyer of the universe.
- **Atman as both one and different from Brahman**: The individual self (Atman) is both one and different from Brahman.
- **Bhakti**: Liberation (moksha) can be achieved through the path of devotion (bhakti) to Radha and Krishna.

## 6. Key Contributions

- **Systematization of Achintya Bheda Abheda**: Chaitanya Mahaprabhu systematized and consolidated the teachings of Achintya Bheda Abheda, making it one of the most influential schools of Hindu philosophy.
- **Establishment of Gaudiya Vaishnavism**: Chaitanya Mahaprabhu established Gaudiya Vaishnavism, a devotional tradition that has millions of followers around the world.

## 7. Significance

Chaitanya Mahaprabhu is a towering figure in the history of Hindu philosophy. His teachings have had a profound and lasting impact on the intellectual and spiritual landscape of Hinduism, and he is revered as a saint and a philosopher by millions of people around the world.

## 8. Relationship to Other Traditions

Chaitanya Mahaprabhu's teachings are a synthesis of the dualistic and non-dualistic schools of Vedanta. He was a critic of the impersonalism of Advaita Vedanta and the dualism of Dvaita.
