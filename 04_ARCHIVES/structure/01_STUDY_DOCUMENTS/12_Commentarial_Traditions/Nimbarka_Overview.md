## 1. Introduction

**Nimbarka** (c. 13th century CE) was a Hindu philosopher and theologian who founded the **Dvaitadvaita** (dualistic non-dualism) school of Vedanta. He is also the founder of the **Nimbarka Sampradaya**, a devotional tradition that emphasizes the worship of Radha and Krishna.

## 2. Biographical Information

- **Birth**: Mungi, Maharashtra, India
- **Life**: Nimbarka is said to have been a child prodigy who mastered the Vedas and other scriptures at a very young age. He traveled extensively throughout India, engaging in debates with scholars of other philosophical schools and propagating his teachings.

## 3. Philosophical School: Dvaitadvaita

Dvaitadvaita is a dualistic non-dualistic school of Vedanta that holds that the ultimate reality (Brahman) is both one and many. The individual self (Atman) is both identical with and different from Brahman.

## 4. Major Works

Nimbarka wrote a commentary on the Brahma Sutras (the **Vedanta Parijata Saurabha**). He also wrote a number of independent philosophical works, including the **Dashashloki**.

## 5. Core Teachings

- **Brahman as both one and many**: The ultimate reality (Brahman) is both one and many. It is one in the sense that it is the ultimate source of all reality, and it is many in the sense that it manifests itself in a variety of forms.
- **Atman as both identical with and different from Brahman**: The individual self (Atman) is both identical with and different from Brahman. It is identical with Brahman in the sense that it is a part of Brahman, but it is different from Brahman in the sense that it is a finite and dependent being.
- **Bhakti**: Liberation (moksha) can be achieved through the path of devotion (bhakti) to Radha and Krishna.

## 6. Key Contributions

- **Systematization of Dvaitadvaita**: Nimbarka systematized and consolidated the teachings of Dvaitadvaita, making it one of the most influential schools of Hindu philosophy.
- **Establishment of the Nimbarka Sampradaya**: Nimbarka established the Nimbarka Sampradaya, a devotional tradition that has millions of followers around the world.

## 7. Significance

Nimbarka is a towering figure in the history of Hindu philosophy. His teachings have had a profound and lasting impact on the intellectual and spiritual landscape of Hinduism, and he is revered as a saint and a philosopher by millions of people around the world.

## 8. Relationship to Other Traditions

Nimbarka engaged in debates with scholars of other philosophical schools, including Advaita Vedanta and Dvaita. He was a critic of the monism of Advaita Vedanta and the dualism of Dvaita.
