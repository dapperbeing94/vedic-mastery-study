## 1. Introduction

**Sriharsha** (c. 12th century CE) was a Hindu philosopher and poet who is known for his work on Advaita Vedanta. He is considered one of the most important thinkers in the history of Indian philosophy, and his works have had a profound and lasting impact on the intellectual and spiritual landscape of Hinduism.

## 2. Biographical Information

- **Birth**: Kannauj, Uttar Pradesh, India
- **Life**: Sriharsha was a court poet of the Gahadavala king Jayachandra. He is said to have been a master of both poetry and philosophy, and he is revered as a saint and a philosopher by millions of people around the world.

## 3. Philosophical School: Advaita Vedanta

Sriharsha was a proponent of Advaita Vedanta, a non-dualistic school of Hindu philosophy that posits the ultimate reality (Brahman) as the only reality. The individual self (Atman) is identical with Brahman, and the perceived multiplicity of the world is an illusion (maya).

## 4. Major Works

Sriharsha is the author of the **Khandanakhandakhadya**, a dialectical work that refutes the doctrines of other philosophical schools. He also wrote the **Naishadha Charita**, a mahakavya (epic poem) that is considered one of the masterpieces of Sanskrit literature.

## 5. Core Teachings

- **Brahman as the only reality**: The ultimate reality is Brahman, which is described as sat-chit-ananda (being-consciousness-bliss).
- **Atman is Brahman**: The individual self (Atman) is identical with Brahman.
- **Maya**: The perceived multiplicity of the world is an illusion (maya) that is superimposed on Brahman.

## 6. Key Contributions

- **Defense of Advaita Vedanta**: Sriharsha was a brilliant defender of Advaita Vedanta, and his Khandanakhandakhadya is a masterpiece of dialectical reasoning.
- **Poetry**: Sriharsha was also a great poet, and his Naishadha Charita is one of the most celebrated works of Sanskrit literature.

## 7. Significance

Sriharsha is a towering figure in the history of Indian philosophy. His teachings have had a profound and lasting impact on the intellectual and spiritual landscape of Hinduism, and he is revered as a saint and a philosopher by millions of people around the world.

## 8. Relationship to Other Traditions

Sriharsha engaged in debates with scholars of other philosophical schools, including Nyaya and Vaisheshika. He was a fierce critic of the realism of these schools.
