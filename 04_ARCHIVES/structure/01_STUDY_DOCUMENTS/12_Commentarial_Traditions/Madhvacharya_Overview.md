## 1. Introduction

**Madhvacharya** (c. 1238-1317 CE) was a prominent Hindu philosopher and theologian who founded the **Dvaita** (dualistic) school of Vedanta. He is considered one of the most important thinkers in the history of Hindu philosophy, and his teachings have had a profound and lasting impact on the intellectual and spiritual landscape of Hinduism.

## 2. Biographical Information

- **Birth**: Pajaka, near Udupi, Karnataka, India
- **Life**: Madhvacharya was a student of Achyutapreksha, but he later broke with his teacher and developed his own philosophical system. He traveled extensively throughout India, engaging in debates with scholars of other philosophical schools and propagating his teachings.

## 3. Philosophical School: Dvaita

Dvaita is a dualistic school of Vedanta that holds that the ultimate reality (Brahman) is a personal God (Vishnu) who is distinct from the individual self (Atman) and the material world. The individual self is a dependent and finite being, while Brahman is an independent and infinite being.

## 4. Major Works

Madhvacharya wrote commentaries on the Brahma Sutras, the Bhagavad Gita, and the Upanishads. He also wrote a number of independent philosophical works, including the **Anuvyakhyana** and the **Mahabharata Tatparya Nirnaya**.

## 5. Core Teachings

- **Brahman as a personal God**: The ultimate reality is a personal God (Vishnu) who is the creator, preserver, and destroyer of the universe.
- **Atman as distinct from Brahman**: The individual self (Atman) is distinct from Brahman and is a dependent and finite being.
- **Bhakti**: Liberation (moksha) can be achieved through the path of devotion (bhakti) to Vishnu.

## 6. Key Contributions

- **Systematization of Dvaita**: Madhvacharya systematized and consolidated the teachings of Dvaita, making it one of the most influential schools of Hindu philosophy.
- **Establishment of the Udupi Sri Krishna Matha**: Madhvacharya established the Udupi Sri Krishna Matha, which has been a major center of Dvaita Vedanta for centuries.

## 7. Significance

Madhvacharya is a towering figure in the history of Hindu philosophy. His teachings have had a profound and lasting impact on the intellectual and spiritual landscape of Hinduism, and he is revered as a saint and a philosopher by millions of people around the world.

## 8. Relationship to Other Traditions

Madhvacharya engaged in debates with scholars of other philosophical schools, including Advaita Vedanta and Vishishtadvaita. He was a fierce critic of the monism of Advaita Vedanta and the qualified monism of Vishishtadvaita.
