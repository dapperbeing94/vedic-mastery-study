## Liberation (Moká¹£a)

Liberation in Vaisheshika is achieved through:

## Historical Influence

**Scientific Naturalism**: Vaisheshika represents one of the earliest systematic attempts at natural philosophy and proto-scientific thinking in India.

**Atomic Theory**: Its atomic theory predates Western atomic theories by many centuries and shows sophisticated understanding of matter and causation.

**Categorization**: Its systematic categorization of reality influenced all subsequent Indian philosophy and provided a framework for analyzing existence.

**Cross-Cultural Dialogue**: Vaisheshika atomism has been compared with Greek atomism (Democritus, Leucippus) and modern atomic theory, though significant differences exist.

## Modern Relevance

2. **Metaphysics**: Its categorical analysis offers insights into ontology and substance theory
3. **Comparative Philosophy**: Provides non-Western perspective on atomism and natural philosophy
4. **Environmental Philosophy**: Its respect for natural elements has ecological implications
