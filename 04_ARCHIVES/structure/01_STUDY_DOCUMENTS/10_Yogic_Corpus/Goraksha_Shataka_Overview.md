## 1. Introduction

The **Goraksha Shataka** (also known as the Goraksha Paddhati) is a foundational Sanskrit text of Hatha Yoga, attributed to the great sage **Gorakhnath**. It is a concise and practical manual that outlines the key practices of the Nath tradition of yoga.

## 2. Author and Lineage

- **Author**: Gorakhnath, a revered yogi and the founder of the Nath Sampradaya.
- **Date**: The exact date of composition is uncertain, but it is believed to be a medieval text, likely composed between the 10th and 12th centuries.

## 3. Structure

The Goraksha Shataka is a short text, consisting of approximately 100 verses (shlokas). It is organized into a single chapter that covers the essential practices of Hatha Yoga in a systematic manner.

## 4. Core Teachings

The central teaching of the Goraksha Shataka is the attainment of Samadhi through the practice of Hatha Yoga. The text emphasizes the importance of purifying the body and mind, controlling the breath, and awakening the Kundalini energy. It also highlights the role of the guru in guiding the disciple on the path of yoga.

## 5. Key Practices

- **Asanas**: The text describes two primary asanas: Siddhasana and Padmasana.
- **Pranayama**: Various breathing techniques for purifying the nadis and controlling the prana.
- **Mudras and Bandhas**: Psycho-physical gestures and energy locks for awakening Kundalini.
- **Samadhi**: The ultimate goal of meditative absorption.

## 6. Significance

The Goraksha Shataka is significant as one of the earliest and most authoritative texts of the Nath tradition. It provides a clear and concise summary of the essential practices of Hatha Yoga and has been a major influence on later texts, including the Hatha Yoga Pradipika.

## 7. Relationship to Other Texts

The Goraksha Shataka is a foundational text that has influenced many later works on Hatha Yoga. It is often studied alongside other texts of the Nath tradition, such as the **Siddha Siddhanta Paddhati** and the **Viveka Martanda**.
