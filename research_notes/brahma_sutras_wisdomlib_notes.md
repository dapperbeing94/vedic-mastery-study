## Summary

English translation of the Brahma sutras (aka. Vedanta Sutras) with commentary by Shankaracharya (Shankara Bhashya): One of the three canonical texts of the Vedanta school of Hindu philosophy. The Brahma sutra is the exposition of the philosophy of the Upanishads. It is an attempt to systematise the various strands of the Upanishads which form the background of the orthodox systems of thought.

## Structure

The text is organized into:
- **Four Adhyāyas** (chapters)
- Each Adhyāya has **Four Pādas** (quarters)
- Total of 555 sutras organized systematically

### First Adhyāya (Samanvaya - Harmony)
- 4 Pādas with 31, 32, 42, and 28 sutras respectively

### Second Adhyāya (Avirodha - Non-Contradiction)
- 4 Pādas with 37, 45, 53, and 22 sutras respectively

### Third Adhyāya (Sādhana - Means)
- 4 Pādas with 27, 40, 66, and 52 sutras respectively

### Fourth Adhyāya (Phala - Fruit)
- 4 Pādas with 19, 17, 16, and 22 sutras respectively
