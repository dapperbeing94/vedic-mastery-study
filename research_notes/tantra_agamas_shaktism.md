# Shaktism and Shakta Tantras - Wikipedia Research

**Source**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shaktism

## Overview of Shaktism

**Shaktism** is one of the major denominations of Hinduism, wherein the metaphysical reality (Brahman) is considered feminine (Devi), and the Devi is regarded as the Supreme Being. Shaktism is closely related with Tantric Hinduism, which teaches rituals and practices for purification of the mind and body.

## Core Beliefs

- **Shakti** (divine feminine energy/power) is the Supreme Being
- The Devi is both transcendent and immanent
- Emphasis on the worship of the Goddess in her various forms
- Integration of Tantric practices for spiritual realization

## Major Shakta Tantras

The Shakta tradition has numerous Tantric texts, including:

1. **Devi Mahatmya** (Glory of the Goddess) - Part of Markandeya Purana
2. **Devi Bhagavata Purana** - Major Shakta Purana
3. **Lalita Sahasranama** - 1000 names of Goddess Lalita
4. **Soundarya Lahari** - Attributed to Adi Shankara
5. **Tantrasara** - Compendium of Tantric practices
6. **Kularnava Tantra** - Important Kaula tradition text
7. **Mahanirvana Tantra** - Influential Shakta Tantra
8. **Yogini Tantra** - Associated with Kamakhya temple

## The Ten Mahavidyas

The **Dasha Mahavidyas** (Ten Great Wisdom Goddesses) are central to Shakta Tantra:

1. **Kali** - Goddess of time and death
2. **Tara** - Goddess who guides through troubles
3. **Tripura Sundari (Shodashi)** - Goddess of beauty and perfection
4. **Bhuvaneshwari** - Goddess of the world
5. **Chinnamasta** - Self-decapitated goddess
6. **Bhairavi** - Fierce goddess
7. **Dhumavati** - Widow goddess
8. **Bagalamukhi** - Goddess who paralyzes enemies
9. **Matangi** - Goddess of inner thought
10. **Kamala** - Lotus goddess

## Tantric Practices in Shaktism

- **Mantra** - Sacred sound formulas
- **Yantra** - Sacred geometric diagrams
- **Puja** - Ritual worship
- **Kundalini Yoga** - Awakening the serpent power
- **Chakra meditation** - Working with energy centers
- **Panchamakara** - Five M's ritual (controversial practices)

## Schools of Shaktism

1. **Kaula** - Emphasizes freedom and direct experience
2. **Mishra** - Mixed tradition
3. **Samaya** - Emphasizes internal worship and Vedic orthodoxy
4. **Trika** - Kashmir Shaivism's Shakta aspect

## Relationship to Tantra

Shaktism is deeply intertwined with Tantric Hinduism. The Shakta Tantras provide:
- Ritual frameworks for worship
- Esoteric practices for spiritual transformation
- Philosophical foundations for understanding Shakti
- Methods for harnessing divine feminine energy

## Geographic Centers

- **Kamakhya Temple** (Assam) - Major Shakta pilgrimage site
- **Dakshineswar Kali Temple** (West Bengal)
- **Kalighat** (Kolkata)
- Various **Shakti Peethas** across India

## Historical Development

- Ancient roots in goddess worship traditions
- Systematized during medieval period (500-1500 CE)
- Integration with Vedantic philosophy
- Influence from Kashmir Shaivism
- Modern revival through figures like Ramakrishna and Vivekananda

## Key Concepts

- **Shakti-Shiva Unity**: Shakti (energy) and Shiva (consciousness) are inseparable
- **Prakriti-Purusha**: Matter and spirit as divine feminine and masculine
- **Maya**: The creative power of the Goddess
- **Kundalini**: The dormant spiritual energy at the base of the spine
