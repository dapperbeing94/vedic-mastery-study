## Key Information

**Title**: Kena Upanishad (केन उपनिषद्) or Kenopanishad (केनोपनिषत्)

**Summary**: The Kena Upanishad is a collection of philosophical poems discussing the attributes of Brahman: the unchanging, infinite universal spirit. Brahman is further proposed as the cause for all the forces of nature, symbolized as Gods.

**Commentary**: This translation includes Shankara's commentary focusing on 'Advaita Vedanta', or non-dualism: one of the classical orthodox philosophies of Hinduism.

**Translation**: By S. Sitarama Sastri (1905), 13,003 words

**Format**: Includes original Sanskrit text and transliterated roman text in IAST format.

## Key Themes

- The nature of Brahman as the ultimate cause
- The relationship between Brahman and the forces of nature (symbolized as Gods)
- Non-dualism (Advaita Vedanta)
- The inquiry into "By whom" (Kena) the mind and senses function
