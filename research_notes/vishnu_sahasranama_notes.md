# Vishnu Sahasranama Research Notes

## Source
Swami Krishnananda's website - Divine Life Society
URL: https://www.swami-krishnananda.org/vishnu/vishnu_1.html

## Key Information

### Structure
- Found in Mahabharata, Anushasana Parva
- Contains 1000 names of Lord Vishnu
- Organized in slokas (verses)
- Each sloka contains multiple names
- Shankaracharya wrote comprehensive commentary (Bhasya)

### Format
- Sanskrit text in Devanagari
- Transliteration in IAST
- English translations of each name
- Meanings and significance explained

### Sample Names (First Sloka)
1. Viśvam - The all or the Universe
2. Viṣṇuḥ - He who pervades everything
3. Vaṣaṭkāraḥ - For whom the sacrificial versus are uttered in the yajnas
4. Bhūta-bhavya-bhavat-prabhuḥ - The one who is the master and beyond the past, present and the future
5. Bhūtakṛd - The creator and destroyer of all existences in the universe
6. Bhūtabhṛd - One who supports or sustains or governs the universe

### Significance
- Daily recitation practice in Hindu tradition
- Devotional hymn (stotra)
- Each name reveals divine attributes
- Used for meditation and worship
- Shankaracharya's commentary provides deep philosophical insights

### Cross-References
- Part of Mahabharata epic
- Connected to Bhagavad Gita teachings
- Relates to Upanishadic concepts of Brahman
- Vishnu as supreme deity in Vaishnavism

### Authoritative Sources
- Shankaracharya's Bhasya (commentary)
- Divine Life Society publications
- Multiple scholarly translations available
