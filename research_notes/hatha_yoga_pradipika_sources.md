# Hatha Yoga Pradipika - Research Sources

## Primary Source Accessed
- **Sacred Texts Archive**: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/hyp/index.htm
- Translation by Pancham Sinh (1914)
- Includes Sanskrit (Devanagari and romanization) with English translation
- Public domain text

## Key Information Confirmed
- **Author**: Swami Swatmarama, disciple of Swami Goraknath
- **Date**: 15th century CE
- **Tradition**: Natha Sampradaya
- **Structure**: Four chapters (Upadeshas)
  1. Chapter I: On Āsanas
  2. Chapter II: On Prāṇāyāma
  3. Chapter III: On Mudrās
  4. Chapter IV: On Samādhi

## Additional Sources to Consult
- Archive.org: Full Sanskrit text with English translation
- Jyotsnā commentary by Brahmānanda
- Translation by Swami Muktibodhananda (Bihar School of Yoga)
- Scholarly works by James Mallinson and Mark Singleton

## Notes
- Text explicitly positions Hatha Yoga as preparatory for Raja Yoga
- Goal is Samādhi through awakening Kundalini
- Emphasizes purification of body and control of prāṇa
- Ha (sun/prāṇa) + Tha (moon/apāna) = union of opposing forces
